## Technical Boundaries — How This Is Enforced

This isn't a policy — it's enforced in code. Here's how:

### 1. Two completely separate authentication systems
- **Owner / God-Mode:** uses the platform master key, only valid via internal 
  admin URLs. Master key is stored as a server environment variable; the 
  browser never receives it from a server response. The owner pastes it once 
  and the browser stores it locally on the owner's device only.
- **HR Admin Portal:** uses a 30-day cryptographically-signed token (HMAC-SHA256). 
  The token is bound to **one company ID** at creation time and cannot be 
  modified, replayed against another company, or extended.

### 2. Server-side scoping on every query
Every database query made by an HR admin is automatically filtered by the 
company ID embedded in their signed token. There is no API parameter an HR 
admin can pass to query another company. Even if an attacker forged a request 
with a different company ID in the URL, the server ignores the URL parameter 
and uses only the cryptographically-verified token claim.

### 3. The 5-employee aggregate threshold
This is enforced **server-side**. The dashboard simply doesn't receive scores 
until the threshold is met — there's nothing to "unhide" in the browser.

### 4. The owner's master key cannot be used on the HR portal endpoints
The HR portal API requires the signed company token specifically and refuses 
the master key. This is by design: even Whitney cannot accidentally land in 
your HR view and confuse data. Owner access uses a different URL and a 
different code path.

### 5. Audit logging
The following actions are logged with timestamp, actor identity, and target:
- HR admin login (success and failure)
- Employee removal
- Owner/master-key access to any company
- Any data export or report generation

Audit logs are append-only and retained for 7 years.

# NeuroQuest Zen Pro — How We Detect Burnout
## A Plain-English Guide for HR Leaders

This document explains how NeuroQuest identifies burnout risk in your workforce, 
why our approach catches it weeks earlier than traditional HR signals, and how 
the platform actively guides each affected employee back toward recovery — 
all without HR ever seeing an individual's score.

Print this for your leadership team. It's the document that turns skeptical 
executives into believers.

---

## Why Traditional HR Burnout Detection Fails

By the time burnout shows up in conventional HR data, the damage is already 
done. The typical HR signals are **trailing indicators** — they tell you about 
problems you can no longer prevent.

| Traditional HR Signal | Why It's Too Late |
|---|---|
| Resignation letter | The decision was made 6–12 weeks earlier |
| PTO spike or sick-day pattern | Person is already in active recovery — or worse, presenteeism |
| Drop in performance review scores | Surfaces 3–6 months after the underlying decline |
| Annual engagement survey dip | Cadence is far too slow; results are anonymized away from action |
| Exit interview themes | By definition, they're leaving |
| Manager 1:1 reports | Highly variable; depends on manager skill and trust |

**The pattern:** every traditional signal fires after the employee has 
**already mentally checked out**. The cost — replacement, lost productivity, 
team morale damage — is already locked in.

---

## What We Watch Instead — Leading Indicators

NeuroQuest's burnout detection runs on **leading indicators** that show up 
4–8 weeks before any traditional HR metric would flag a problem.

These are signals an employee unconsciously generates while using the platform 
during normal voluntary engagement. The employee doesn't have to fill out a 
survey. They don't have to self-report. They just play the wellness games — and 
the data emerges naturally.

### The signal categories we track

1. **Cognitive performance trajectory** 
   How quickly an employee's focus, reaction time, and pattern-recognition 
   scores change session-over-session. A flattening or declining curve is one 
   of the earliest signs of cognitive fatigue.

2. **Mood-state volatility** 
   Not just *what* mood they report, but how *unstable* it is across days. 
   Stable low-mood is one pattern; high-amplitude swings between sessions is a 
   different (and often more concerning) pattern.

3. **Engagement cadence drift** 
   Changes in *when* they engage. Drift toward late-night sessions, weekend 
   sessions replacing weekday, or session length collapsing from 8 minutes to 
   90 seconds — each is a meaningful signal.

4. **Recovery-response signature** 
   How well an employee bounces back from a low day. Healthy individuals 
   recover within 1–2 sessions; at-risk individuals show flat or worsening 
   recovery curves.

5. **Engagement withdrawal patterns** 
   Sudden multi-day absences after a previously consistent pattern. This is 
   often the strongest single predictor.

---

## Our Triple-Weighted Algorithm (Plain English)

We don't trust any single signal. Burnout is a multi-dimensional state, so we 
combine signals across three weighted dimensions and only flag when all three 
align.

### Dimension 1 — **Cognitive Load Signal** *(weight: highest)*
Built from cognitive-performance trajectory and recovery-response signature. 
Captures whether the brain is getting more or less efficient under task. When 
this dimension elevates, it means the person's cognitive resources are being 
depleted faster than they're being replenished.

### Dimension 2 — **Affective State Signal** *(weight: medium)*
Built from mood-state volatility and self-reported affect data. Captures the 
emotional regulation system. Sustained instability here means the buffering 
capacity for stress is wearing thin.

### Dimension 3 — **Behavioral Pattern Signal** *(weight: contextual)*
Built from engagement cadence drift and withdrawal patterns. Captures the 
behavioral expression of internal state. We weight this contextually — it's a 
strong confirmer when the other two dimensions are also elevated, but on its 
own it can have benign explanations (vacation, schedule change).

### How they combine

The three dimensions are blended into a single 0–100 risk score using a 
proprietary weighting that adjusts based on:
- The individual's personal baseline (we never compare across people; we 
  compare each person to their own normal)
- Their tenure on the platform (early signals are weighted differently than 
  established-baseline signals)
- The temporal coherence between dimensions (alignment increases confidence)

> *We don't share the exact weighting math — that's our edge. What we do 
> share: the algorithm is regularly validated against clinical research on

## How We Guide Employees Toward Recovery

Detection without intervention is just surveillance. The whole point is 
**recovery**, and the platform handles this automatically — without HR ever 
needing to know who is being supported.

### What happens when an employee crosses into Moderate risk

1. **Tone of in-app coaching shifts** — from performance/challenge framing to 
   restoration/replenishment framing
2. **Game recommendations change** — toward parasympathetic-activating exercises 
   (breath work, gentle focus, recovery games) and away from high-stimulation 
   challenges
3. **Session length suggestions adjust** — shorter, gentler sessions
4. **Educational micro-content surfaces** — small evidence-based articles on 
   recovery, sleep, boundaries
5. **A check-in prompt appears** — gentle, optional, non-clinical

### What happens when an employee crosses into High risk

All of the above, plus:

6. **Daily restoration plan** generated specifically for their pattern
7. **Resource-finder activates** — surfaces EAP information if their employer 
   provided one, plus general crisis lines
8. **Encouragement to rest** — the app actively suggests skipping a session if 
   the data suggests cognitive depletion is severe
9. **Optional self-referral** — the user can opt in to schedule with a licensed 
   professional through partner networks (no HR involvement, employee-paid or 
   EAP-paid)

**Throughout all of this:** HR sees only that the *aggregate* high-risk 
percentage moved. They never know who. The employee is supported privately, 
with full dignity preserved.

## How HR Watches Resilience Build

Your HR Dashboard at `/company-admin` shows the **inverse** of burnout: the 
**Workforce Resilience Index (WRI)**.

### The WRI is the metric to watch

| WRI Score | What's Happening |
|---|---|
| 0–40 | Significant strain across the workforce |
| 41–60 | Building — typical pilot starting range |
| 61–75 | Healthy — sustained good performance |
| 76–100 | Thriving — high-performance team state |

### What you'll see on the dashboard

- **Current WRI** — a single number with a color-coded badge
- **14-day trend line** — direction matters more than the absolute value
- **Burnout risk distribution** — the % of your team in Low / Moderate / High 
  tiers (only visible when 5+ employees are active)
- **Top contributing factors** — generalized themes (e.g. "engagement cadence 
  declining," "cognitive recovery times improving") with no individual 
  attribution
- **Projected trajectory** — where the curve is heading 7 and 30 days out

### What to celebrate

- WRI rising +5 points in a 30-day window = strong, real progress
- High-risk percentage dropping by ≥30% from baseline = exceptional outcome
- Any sustained two-week upward trend = the program is taking hold

### What triggers an account-manager call

- WRI dropping more than 5 points in 14 days
- High-risk percentage rising above 18%
- Engagement collapse (active rate falling below 30% for two consecutive weeks)

In all cases, the conversation is about **what the organization can change**, 
never about which individuals are struggling.
