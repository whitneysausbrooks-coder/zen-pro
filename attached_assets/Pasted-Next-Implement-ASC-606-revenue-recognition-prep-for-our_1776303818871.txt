Next: Implement ASC 606 revenue recognition prep for our per-seat B2B subscriptions ($12/seat/month).

Apply the five-step ASC 606 model: identify contract, performance obligations (stand-ready access satisfied over time), determine transaction price, allocate, and recognize ratably as service is provided. Focus on practical SaaS realities with our existing Stripe integration (webhooks already sync subscription_status, seat_count, billing_period_end, etc.).

Key requirements:

1. **Data Model**:
   - Extend the companies table or add a lightweight revenue_schedules table (linked by company_id + subscription_id) to track:
     - Transaction price (from Stripe)
     - Recognized revenue to date (MTD, YTD, lifetime)
     - Remaining deferred/unearned revenue balance
     - Current daily/monthly recognition rate (adjusted for seat_count)
     - Recognition period start/end dates

2. **Recognition Logic** (ratable/straight-line over time):
   - Recognize revenue daily (or monthly) over the billing period using data from Stripe.
   - For a monthly period: divide the period amount by number of days and recognize proportionally.
   - Make Stripe the source of truth for billing events; compute recognition in our system for fast dashboard/reporting.

3. **Handle Edge Cases for Zero Defects** (critical):
   - Mid-cycle seat changes (/update-seats): Treat as prospective contract modification — recalculate the remaining recognition rate based on new seat_count for the rest of the period (no retroactive adjustment, as additional seats are distinct).
   - Proration: Automatically handle partial periods on new subs, upgrades, or cancellations.
   - Cancellations: Stop recognition after the effective end date; handle remaining deferred revenue per policy (typically release if non-refundable).
   - Refunds/credits or past_due: Adjust deferred/recognized amounts appropriately.
   - True-ups/upgrades/downgrades: Update schedules prospectively.

4. **Daily Recognition Job** (cron):
   - Run daily to process active subscriptions, calculate recognized revenue for the day, update balances, and record details (journal-entry style) for audit.

5. **Dashboard & Reporting**:
   - Add/enhance views or endpoints for:
     - Recognized revenue (MTD, YTD, by company)
     - Current deferred revenue balance
     - Simple revenue waterfall (by period/month)
     - Exportable CSV report for finance/audits

6. **Audit & Logging**:
   - Log every recognition run, schedule change, or adjustment in audit_logs (amount, reason, before/after balances, linked to Stripe event where possible).

7. **Testing**:
   - Comprehensive tests for: new subscription, mid-month seat increase (e.g., add seats on day 15 of 30-day month), cancellation mid-period, proration, refund, and basic annual case if supported.
   - Verify math aligns with ratable recognition (e.g., 5 seats @ $12/month = $60/month spread daily).

Keep it efficient, integrated with existing webhook + companies flow, and focused on prospective treatment for modifications. Aim for clean GAAP-compliant visibility without external revenue tools yet.
