Next: Implement ASC 606 revenue recognition prep for our per-seat B2B subscriptions ($12/seat/month).

Apply the five-step ASC 606 model with focus on practical SaaS realities: performance obligation is stand-ready access satisfied over time (ratable/straight-line recognition). Contract modifications like mid-cycle seat changes are treated prospectively in most cases (additional seats are distinct and priced at standalone selling price).

Key requirements, building on our existing hardened Stripe integration (webhooks sync subscription_status, seat_count, billing_period_end, etc.):

1. **Data Model**:
   - Extend the companies table or add a lightweight revenue_schedules table (linked by company_id + subscription_id) to track per subscription:
     - Transaction price (from Stripe)
     - Recognized revenue to date (MTD, YTD, lifetime)
     - Remaining deferred/unearned revenue balance
     - Current daily/monthly recognition rate (adjusted for seat_count)
     - Recognition period start/end dates (tied to billing periods)

2. **Recognition Logic** (ratable over time):
   - Recognize revenue straight-line/daily over the billing period using data from Stripe.
   - Example: Monthly period amount divided by days in period, recognized proportionally each day.
   - For simplicity and auditability, prefer daily precision where practical.

3. **Handle Edge Cases for Zero Defects**:
   - Mid-cycle seat changes (/update-seats): Treat as prospective contract modification — recalculate the remaining recognition rate based on new seat_count for the rest of the period (no retroactive adjustment or catch-up, as additional seats are distinct).
   - Proration: Automatically handle partial periods on new subs, upgrades, or cancellations.
   - Cancellations: Stop recognition after the effective end date; handle remaining deferred revenue per policy (typically release if non-refundable).
   - Refunds/credits or past_due: Adjust deferred/recognized amounts appropriately.
   - True-ups, upgrades/downgrades: Update schedules prospectively based on new seat_count and remaining term.

4. **Daily Recognition Job** (cron):
   - Run daily to process active subscriptions, calculate recognized revenue for the day, update balances, and record journal-entry-style details for audit.

5. **Dashboard & Reporting**:
   - Add/enhance views or endpoints for:
     - Recognized revenue (MTD, YTD, by company)
     - Current deferred revenue balance
     - Simple revenue waterfall (by period/month)
     - Exportable CSV report for finance/audits

6. **Audit & Logging**:
   - Log every recognition run, schedule change, or adjustment in audit_logs (include amount, reason, before/after balances, linked to Stripe event where possible).

7. **Testing**:
   - Comprehensive tests covering: new monthly subscription, mid-month seat increase (e.g., add seats on day 15 of a 30-day month), cancellation mid-period, proration, refund, and basic annual case if supported.
   - Verify math aligns with ratable recognition (e.g., 5 seats @ $12/month = $60/month spread daily over the period).

Keep implementation efficient, integrated with existing webhook + companies flow, and focused on prospective treatment for modifications. Aim for clean, accurate GAAP/ASC 606-compliant financial visibility without external revenue recognition tools yet. Prioritize audit-readiness and zero defects on seat changes/proration.
