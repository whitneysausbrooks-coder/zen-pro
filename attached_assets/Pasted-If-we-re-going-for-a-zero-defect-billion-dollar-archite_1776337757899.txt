• Activity/Strain (15%): This acts as a "modifier." High activity with high HRV is "Growth"; high activity with low HRV is "Burnout Risk."

🔬 Refining the "Neuro-Resilience" Algorithm
To avoid the "one bad night" noise, we should apply a 7-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA). This ensures the score reflects the user's trend rather than a single outlier, which is vital for corporate wellness—you don't want an employee panicking over one late night.
The Component Breakdown
• HRV (50%): We use the \bm{ln(RMSSD)} (Root Mean Square of Successive Differences). This normalizes the high variance in raw heart rate data.
• Sleep (35%): We calculate a "Sleep Efficiency" coefficient: \bm{\frac{\text{Deep} + \text{REM}}{\text{Total Time in Bed}}}.
• Strain (15%): This is a metabolic penalty. If Strain > Recovery (HRV + Sleep), the score should reflect a "Functional Overreach" warning.
