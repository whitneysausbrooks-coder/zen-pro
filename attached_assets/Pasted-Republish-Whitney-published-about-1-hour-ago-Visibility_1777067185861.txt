Here's the fail-proof Apple review playbook, written specifically for NeuroQuest's setup (HealthKit, enterprise invite-code identity, wellness positioning, expo-iap installed). The rejection rate jumps when even one of these is missing — getting them all right typically clears review on the first submission.

1. The Five Things That Will Get You Rejected on This App Specifically
These are the highest-probability rejection reasons for your combination of features:

a) HealthKit usage strings that aren't specific. In app.json, the NSHealthShareUsageDescription must explain exactly what you read and why — and it must mention each type. Reviewers reject generic strings like "to track your health." Use something like:

"NeuroQuest reads your Heart Rate Variability, Sleep, and Step Count to compute your daily Neuro Resilience Score. Your data stays on your device and is only shared with your employer in fully-anonymized aggregates of 5+ employees."
b) Account deletion missing. Since June 2022, any app with account creation must offer in-app account deletion (not just a "contact us" link). Add a "Delete my account" button in Profile that wipes the user from enterprise_users server-side and clears AsyncStorage.

c) Sign in with Apple. Guideline 4.8: if you offer any third-party social login, you must also offer Sign in with Apple. You currently use email + invite_code (not social) — this is safe because invite_code is a "first-party shared secret," but document it that way in review notes so the reviewer doesn't misread it as social login.

d) Therapeutic / medical claims. "Brain training," "resilience," "wellness" are fine. "Treats anxiety," "diagnoses burnout," "clinical-grade" anywhere user-facing will trigger a medical device escalation that can take weeks. Audit your in-app copy and App Store description for any word a reviewer could read as a treatment claim. Add a footer disclaimer: "NeuroQuest is a wellness tool, not a medical device. Not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Consult a healthcare professional for medical advice."

e) IAP misconfiguration. You have expo-iap installed. If the app sells anything to consumers (subscriptions, unlocks, etc.) it MUST go through Apple IAP. Your B2B model (enterprise pays $50/seat/year via Stripe outside the app) is exempt under guideline 3.1.3(b) — but only if employees never see a paywall in the app. If expo-iap isn't actually selling anything to end users right now, remove the dependency before submitting; reviewers see it and ask "what are you selling?"

5. Submission Strategy
Submit to TestFlight first, not directly to the App Store. Run the build through internal testing for 24–48 hours to catch crashes you'll otherwise discover during review.
Submit Mon–Wed morning Pacific time. Reviews submitted Friday afternoon or weekends sit longer.
Use phased release (in App Store Connect → Version Release) — rolls the update out to 1%, 2%, 5%, 10%, 20%, 50%, 100% over 7 days. If a crash spikes, you halt before all users are hit.
