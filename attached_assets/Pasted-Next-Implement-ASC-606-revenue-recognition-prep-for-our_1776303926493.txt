Follow the five-step ASC 606 model with focus on practical SaaS realities: the performance obligation is stand-ready access to the platform, satisfied over time, so recognize revenue ratably/straight-line. Contract modifications (e.g., mid-cycle seat changes) are typically treated prospectively if additional seats are distinct and priced at standalone selling price (SSP) — no retroactive catch-up in most cases.

Build directly on our existing hardened Stripe integration (webhooks already sync subscription_status, seat_count, billing_period_end, subscription_id, etc.) and companies table.

Key requirements:

1. **Data Model**:
   - Extend companies or add a lightweight revenue_schedules table (linked by company_id + subscription_id) to track:
     - Transaction price (from Stripe)
     - Recognized revenue to date (MTD, YTD, lifetime)
     - Remaining deferred/unearned revenue balance
     - Current daily/monthly recognition rate (adjusted for seat_count)
     - Recognition period start/end dates tied to billing periods

2. **Recognition Logic** (ratable over time):
   - Recognize revenue straight-line/daily over the billing period.
   - Example: For a monthly period, divide the period amount (seats × $12) by days in the period and recognize proportionally each day.
   - Prefer daily precision for accuracy and proration.

3. **Handle Edge Cases for Zero Defects** (critical for audit readiness):
   - Mid-cycle seat changes (/update-seats): Treat as prospective contract modification — recalculate the remaining recognition rate based on new seat_count for the rest of the period (no retroactive adjustment or catch-up, as additional seats are distinct).
   - Proration: Automatically handle partial periods on new subscriptions, upgrades, or cancellations.
   - Cancellations: Stop recognition after the effective end date; handle remaining deferred revenue per policy (typically release if non-refundable).
   - Refunds/credits or past_due: Adjust deferred/recognized amounts appropriately.
   - True-ups, upgrades/downgrades: Update schedules prospectively based on new seat_count and remaining term.

4. **Daily Recognition Job** (cron):
   - Run daily to process active subscriptions, calculate the day's recognized revenue, update balances, and record journal-entry-style details (for audit trail).

5. **Dashboard & Reporting**:
   - Add or enhance views/endpoints for:
     - Recognized revenue (MTD, YTD, by company)
     - Current deferred revenue balance
     - Simple revenue waterfall summary (by period or month)
     - Exportable CSV report for finance/audits

6. **Audit & Logging**:
   - Log every recognition calculation, schedule change, or adjustment in audit_logs (include amount, reason, before/after balances, linked to Stripe event where possible).

7. **Testing**:
   - Comprehensive tests for: new monthly subscription, mid-month seat increase (e.g., add seats on day 15 of a 30-day month — verify prospective rate adjustment only), cancellation mid-period, proration examples, refund handling.
   - Confirm calculations match ratable math (e.g., 5 seats @ $12/month = $60/month spread daily over the period).
